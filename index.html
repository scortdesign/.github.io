GDES 112

Intro to Graphic Design

No prerequisites  ·  UC & CSU transferable

This is your entry point into design. You'll learn how to think visually and work with industry tools like Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop from day one. The course covers color theory, typography, composition, and contrast alongside real projects in branding, packaging, and layout design.

By the end, you'll have a working understanding of the design process: how to sketch an idea, push it through critique, and deliver a polished final piece.

Student Work

Netflix Show Card. Students design a typographic title card for a fictional series, combining custom type treatments with cinematic imagery.

Instagram Ad Campaign. A multi-format campaign built to real social media specs, from carousel ads to urban poster mockups.

GDES 115

Photoshop for Design

No prerequisites  ·  UC & CSU transferable

Adobe Photoshop is the industry standard for image editing, compositing, and digital art. This course teaches you to use it the way professionals do: with non-destructive workflows, smart objects, and adjustment layers that keep your files flexible and production-ready.

Projects cover photo manipulation, campaign design, and working with AI tools as part of your creative process. You'll leave with polished work and a repeatable process you can apply to any brief.

Student Work

Spotify Wrapped Campaign. Students build a full visual identity system for a Spotify Wrapped edition, exploring type, color, and illustration across multiple platforms.

Product Campaign. From brand identity to in-context mockups, students craft cohesive campaigns built around a single product.

GDES 120

Digital Design & AI

Prereq: GDES 112 or 115  ·  UC & CSU transferable

Design today means knowing how to move fast without sacrificing quality. This course teaches modern design workflows in Figma, including components, grids, and reusable systems built for social, web, and presentation contexts.

You'll also learn how to use AI as a genuine creative tool: for concept generation, naming, moodboards, and layout variation. The focus is on authorship. You'll know exactly what you made and why.

Student Work

Brand Identity System. Full brand development from logo and character design through a complete product line, built with Figma and AI-assisted concepting.

Brand Strategy Presentation. Students develop a brand concept from positioning statement through visual identity, then present it as a professional case study.

GDES 122

Graphic Design 2

Prereq: GDES 112  ·  UC & CSU transferable

This is where your design thinking gets pushed further. GDES 122 builds on the fundamentals with more complex briefs: cultural campaigns, packaging systems, and multi-piece identity projects. You'll produce multiple design solutions for each project, learning how to develop and defend a visual direction.

Projects draw from real institutions and industries, so you're solving actual design problems, not hypothetical ones.

Student Work

Packaging and Branding. Students create complete packaging systems with original brand names, logos, typography, and custom illustration applied to a physical product format.

GDES 132

Typography

Prereq: GDES 122  ·  UC & CSU transferable

Type is the foundation of all design. This advanced course is dedicated entirely to typographic practice: how letterforms work, how to set type for readability and impact, and how to use typography as a primary design element rather than an afterthought.

You'll work on editorial layouts, identity systems, and poster design where type carries the weight of the whole piece. If you want to design at a professional level, this course is essential.

Student Work

Event Poster Design. Typographic poster work using real festival and event briefs. Students develop bold, print-ready layouts where type and composition do the heavy lifting.

Editorial Layout. Cover and interior spread design applying typography across multiple page formats and content hierarchies.
